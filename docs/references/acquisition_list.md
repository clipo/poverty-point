## Priority 1 (High) -- 14 of 15 ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED

### Kidder, T.R. & Grooms, S.B. (2024). Chronological hygiene and Bayesian modeling of Poverty Point sites in the Lower Mississippi Valley, circa 4200 to 3200 cal BP.

**DOI:** 10.1017/aaq.2023.85
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** 157 dates, 54% fail hygiene protocol. PP occupation 164-397 years (3535-3135 cal BP). Earthwork construction compressed to ca. 3300-3225 cal BP (~75 years). Rejects unified PP culture.

---

### Grooms, S.B., Ward, G.M.V., & Kidder, T.R. (2023). Convergence at Poverty Point: a revised chronology of the Late Archaic Lower Mississippi Valley.

**DOI:** 10.15184/aqy.2023.100
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** Jaketown had PPOs and imports by 4500-4000 cal BP, 400+ years before PP. Jaketown mounds predate PP mounds by ~100 years. PP is convergence endpoint, not diffusion center. Fall feasting precedes mound construction.

---

### Kidder, T.R. et al. (2021). Multi-method geoarchaeological analyses demonstrates exceptionally rapid construction of Ridge West 3 at Poverty Point.

**DOI:** 10.1080/0734578X.2021.1958445
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** RW3 built with no pauses exceeding days to weeks. Planned fill "recipes." Construction after 3355-3210 cal BP. Fall seasonality inferred.

### Clay, R.B. (2023). Two types of ritual space at the Poverty Point Site 16WC5.

**DOI:** 10.1017/aaq.2023.5
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** Up to 36 timber post circles (earliest in North America), bimodal sizes. Large circles repeatedly built and demolished. 16 sequential activity surfaces at Mound C. Site is "remnant of abandoned projects," not master plan.

---

### Hargrave, M.L. et al. (2021). The complex construction history of Poverty Point's timber circles and concentric ridges.

**DOI:** 10.1080/0734578X.2021.1961350
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** 25.47 ha magnetic survey. 36 timber circles, 64 ridge construction components. Ridge W-1 alone has 17 components. Evidence for deconstruction and fill reuse. Challenges single-episode rapid construction; supports iterative modular building.

---

### Ortmann, A.L. & Kidder, T.R. (2013). Building Mound A at Poverty Point, Louisiana.

**DOI:** 10.1002/gea.21430
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** Mound A (238,500 m3) built in <90 days. ~91,700 person-days; 1,000-3,000 laborers. Micromorphology confirms no weathering between stages. Terminal construction event ca. 3261 cal BP. No hierarchy.

---

### Kidder, T.R., Henry, E.R., & Arco, L.J. (2018). Rapid climate change-induced collapse of hunter-gatherer societies in the lower Mississippi River valley.

**DOI:** 10.1007/s11430-017-9128-8
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Note:** Authors are Kidder, Henry & Arco (not Kidder, Liu & Li as initially listed)
**Key finding:** Crevasse splay buried Jaketown at 3310 cal BP. 530-year abandonment. Post-flood Early Woodland shows no monuments, minimal trade. Monuments as "landesque capital."

---

### Medina-Elizalde, M. et al. (2022). Southeastern United States hydroclimate during Holocene abrupt climate events.

**DOI:** 10.1029/2021PA004346
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** CRITICAL: growth hiatus 4.3-2.6 ka BP means no direct data during PP occupation. Establishes AMOC-wetting mechanism (~90% SE US precipitation increase during North Atlantic cooling). Confirms coherent precipitation variability between Alabama and LMV.

### Sanger, M.C. (2023). Anarchy, institutional flexibility, and containment of authority at Poverty Point (USA).

**DOI:** 10.1080/00438243.2023.2169339
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** Institutional flexibility complementary to signaling model. Authority created during aggregation and dissolved during dispersal. Mandan-Hidatsa buffalo hunting police as best ethnographic analog. Compiles negative evidence against hierarchy.

### Sanger, M.C. (2024). The archaeology of awe: Monumental architecture, communal ritual, and community formation at Poverty Point, USA.

**DOI:** 10.1007/s10816-024-09645-w

### Peacock, E. & Rafferty, J. (2013). The bet-hedging model as an explanatory framework for the evolution of mound building in the southeastern United States.

**DOI:** N/A (book chapter in Beyond Barrows, Sidestone Press, open access)
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** Distinguishes bet-hedging (variable environments) from CST (stable environments). Argues PP is community-level bet-hedging via group selection. Mounds built during stable intervals within variable environments. Table 1 has radiocarbon dates from 13 secure mound contexts. Key theoretical tension with our framework.

### Saunders, J.W. et al. (2005). Watson Brake, a Middle Archaic mound complex in Northeast Louisiana.

**DOI:** 10.2307/40035868 (corrected)
**Status:** ACQUIRED AND PROCESSED
**Key finding:** 11 mounds, ca. 3500-3000 BCE, all local materials, no exchange. Episodic construction with 200+ year hiatuses. Ecotone location parallels PP. 1,300-year gap to PP with no continuity. Authors reject bet-hedging model.

## Gap Notes (Updated)

- **LMV-specific paleoclimate proxy:** CONFIRMED GAP. Medina-Elizalde et al. 2022 has a hiatus during 4.3-2.6 ka BP. Bird et al. 2017 only covers last 2100 years. No high-resolution proxy directly covers PP occupation at PP latitude. Must interpolate from Shuman & Marsicek 2016, Shuman 2024, and the AMOC mechanism from Medina-Elizalde.
- **Ecotone resource quantification:** STILL A GAP. No published study quantifies Macon Ridge ecotone resource diversity or patch covariance.
- **Aggregation population estimates:** PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. Ortmann & Kidder 2013 provide 1,000-3,000 laborer estimate for Mound A. Residential population still poorly constrained.
- **Inter-annual resource variance (sigma):** STILL A GAP. Need ecological data from modern analogs.
- **Post-PP trajectory (3100-2700 BP):** PARTIALLY ADDRESSED. Kidder, Henry & Arco 2018 document 530-year abandonment and complete loss of signaling system. Detailed data on the transition remain sparse.
- **Ward et al. 2022 and Gibson 2000:** Two Priority 1 sources still not acquired.
