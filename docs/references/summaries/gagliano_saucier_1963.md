## Relevance to Argument

This paper documents three Poverty Point-period sites in the Pontchartrain Basin of southeastern Louisiana, extending the geographic range of the Poverty Point cultural sphere into the Gulf coastal deltaic lowlands. The evidence for small, specialized, possibly seasonal camps with broad-spectrum faunal exploitation and long-distance exotic materials directly supports the fission-fusion aggregation model and the role of ecotone diversity and exchange networks in the costly signaling framework.

## Data Presented

- Artifact inventory from three sites (Bayou Jasmine, Linsley, Garcia) with quantified counts in Table 1 (p. 324)
- Four radiocarbon dates from the Linsley site (three accepted, one rejected)
- One geologic radiocarbon date from peat beneath the Linsley site natural levee (2090 +/- 140 B.C.)
- Faunal species list from Linsley site (alligator, alligator gar, rabbit, squirrel, otter, raccoon, muskrat, deer, fish, birds)
- Poverty Point object type frequencies: melon-shaped and biconical forms ~70% at Linsley (contrasting with cylindrical/cross-grooved dominance at the main Poverty Point site)
- Garcia site lithic counts: 95 Jaketown perforators, 79 unused blades, 248 side scrapers, 28 needles, 9 grooved plummets, 1 perforated plummet, 1 grooved boatstone
- Survey data: 150+ sites in the Pontchartrain Basin

## Methodological Notes

- Sites discovered through systematic survey of the Pontchartrain Basin combined with geoarchaeological analysis of Mississippi River subdelta chronology
- Buried sites found via auger boring, linking cultural deposits to geomorphic features (distributary natural levees)
- Radiocarbon dating of charcoal and Rangia shell; one anomalous date (2490 B.C.) excluded from the average without detailed justification beyond its inconsistency with the other three dates
- Artifact classification follows Ford and Webb (1956) typology for Poverty Point objects and lithics
- The Garcia site has no radiocarbon dates; its chronological placement is based on assemblage comparison alone
- Site destruction at the Garcia site due to shoreline retreat means the assemblage is biased (fragile Poverty Point objects likely destroyed by wave action)

- Ford and Webb 1956: Poverty Point type site excavation report; basis for artifact typologies used in comparison

- The broad-spectrum faunal exploitation at Linsley connects to the ecotone diversity hypothesis in the project framework
