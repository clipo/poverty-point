## Relevance to Argument

Pierce 1997 is the most rigorous engineering and evolutionary analysis of Poverty Point objects (PPOs) available. For the costly signaling project, its central contribution is establishing that PPOs are low-cost, expedient, utilitarian cooking implements, not costly signals. Production times of 7-13 seconds per object, use of local clay, and absence of specialized skill eliminate PPOs as candidates for costly signaling. This sharpens the argument: the genuinely costly activities at Poverty Point are monument construction and long-distance exotic goods acquisition, not mundane domestic technology. The paper also demonstrates that PPO shape variation is selectively neutral (size is functionally constrained but shape is not), making shape frequency patterns useful for studying cultural transmission dynamics during aggregation. The massive PPO assemblage at Poverty Point (17,923 objects across all form categories) documents the intensity of domestic activity at the aggregation site, and within-site variation in form frequencies is consistent with multiple groups occupying distinct sectors.

## Data Presented

Pierce presents several quantitative datasets. (1) Production-step counts for 10 PPO form types from the Poverty Point site (Table 11.1), showing 2-5 steps per form. (2) Porosity measurements from 5 biconical PPO fragments from Grey Horse Lake (23PM571), Missouri: mean 42.3%, SD 6.1%. (3) Thermoremanent magnetization data documenting firing/use temperatures of 500-700 degrees C. (4) Digitized perimeter and cross-sectional area measurements from 197 illustrated PPOs across 22+ sites, used to calculate surface-area-to-volume ratios as proxies for heat transfer effectiveness (Figures 11.2-11.5). (5) Assemblage data from 10 lower Mississippi Valley sites totaling 33,435 PPOs classified into 8 form categories (Table 11.2). (6) Form frequencies from multiple collections at the Poverty Point site and J.W. Copes site (Table 11.3), used for within-site reliability assessment.

## Methodological Notes

Pierce applies an explicitly evolutionary framework (Dunnell 1978) to distinguish function (subject to selection) from style (subject to drift). This framework structures the entire analysis: size is identified as functional because it is constrained by thermal performance optimality, while shape is identified as stylistic because it has no measurable effect on performance and displays distributional patterns consistent with drift. The engineering analyses use heat transfer equations and surface-area-to-volume ratios rather than experimental cooking tests, which limits their precision but provides a defensible first-order assessment. The reliance on published illustrations for measurements introduces biases (only complete objects illustrated, shaped forms overrepresented relative to amorphous), which Pierce acknowledges. The chi-square and ANOVA tests are standard. The production-time estimates derive from informal replication rather than controlled experiments, but the order of magnitude (seconds, not minutes or hours) is robust.

- References Ford and Webb 1956 for PPO illustrations and assemblage data from the Poverty Point site.
- Cites Gibson 1974, 1975, 1985 for unpublished thermal experiments and PPO spatial distributions.
- References Hunter 1975 for replication and cooking experiments with PPOs.
- Uses Dunnell 1978 evolutionary framework (selection vs. drift, function vs. style) as the theoretical foundation.
- Grey Horse Lake site data (Dunnell and Whittaker 1990) provides the physical property measurements (porosity, magnetization).
- Gagliano and Saucier 1963 and Ford et al. 1955 referenced for failed seriation attempts.
- Connects to Dunnell and Greenlee 1999 and other evolutionary archaeology studies applying drift models to lower Mississippi Valley assemblages.
- The finding that PPOs are cheap utilitarian goods complements the costly signaling argument built from Ortmann and Kidder 2013 (monument labor costs) and exotic goods sourcing studies.

## Verification Notes

Claims were extracted from a PDF downloaded via ResearchGate. Page numbers correspond to the original volume pagination (pp. 163-190). All quoted passages and data values in the claims file were verified against the PDF. One note on Claim 13: the text states "significant (p > .05)" which appears to be a typographical convention meaning the chi-square result was significant at the .05 level (i.e., the p-value exceeded the critical chi-square value), not that p was greater than .05 in the conventional modern sense. The intended meaning is that differences were statistically significant. Table 11.2 totals were verified against the PDF and are accurate. The production-time data (7-13 seconds) comes from Pierce's own informal replication and should be treated as approximate rather than precise experimental measurements.
