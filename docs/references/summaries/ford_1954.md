## Relevance to Argument

Ford 1954 is the foundational source documenting the discovery and scale of Poverty Point's concentric ridge earthworks, establishing the magnitude of construction labor by preceramic hunter-gatherers that the costly signaling framework seeks to explain. The paper provides the earliest dimensional data for the ridges and Mound A, confirms entirely artificial construction through coring, and identifies the ridges as habitation areas relevant to the aggregation model.

## Data Presented

- Plane table survey measurements of Mound A: 65-70 feet high (1952 survey)
- Bore hole core through Mound A: 60 feet of basket-loaded fill, no natural substratum encountered
- Ridge dimensions from excavation trenches at three points: 4-6 feet high, ~100 feet apart center-to-center
- Motley Mound height: 51 feet (from field observation)
- Macon Ridge elevation: 15-20 feet above Mississippi floodplain
- Octagonal figure diameter: approximately 3/4 mile (~1.2 km)
- Single radiocarbon date from Jaketown (Kulp et al. 1951, sample 114): 2350 +/- 80 years (ca. 400 B.C.), later shown to be too young
- Geomorphological dating based on Fisk (1944): construction estimated at ca. 1500 B.C., erosion of ridges by Arkansas River Stage H at ca. 500 B.C.

## Methodological Notes

Ford combined three lines of evidence: (1) aerial photography from the Mississippi River Commission for identifying earthwork geometry, (2) plane table survey for topographic measurement, and (3) limited excavation (5-foot-wide trenches at three points) and coring (4-inch bore hole, 60 feet deep) for subsurface confirmation. The aerial photographic discovery method is noteworthy because it demonstrates that the earthworks' scale exceeded what ground-level observation could detect. The excavation sample is small (three trenches) and the bore hole provides only a single vertical profile of Mound A, so claims about ridge function and mound composition rest on limited spatial coverage.

## Connections to Other Sources

- **Ortmann and Kidder 2013**: Updated Mound A measurements (~22 m) and construction chronology through modern excavation; confirms Ford's finding of entirely artificial construction
- **Kidder et al. 2008**: Revised understanding of site chronology and singularity of Poverty Point in the archaeological record
- **Fisk 1944**: Geological framework for Macon Ridge and river channel chronology that Ford relies on for dating
- **Gibson 2000**: Comprehensive site monograph building on Ford's foundational documentation
- **Jaketown site (Ford, Phillips, and Haag 1955)**: Related Poverty Point-period site in Mississippi providing the radiocarbon date Ford cites and comparative cultural material

## Verification Notes

- Claims extracted directly from the published text (pp. 282-285). Page numbers match JSTOR journal pagination.
- Mound A height (65-70 feet) is consistent with later measurements; minor differences reflect different survey datums and post-1954 erosion.
- Ford's octagonal interpretation of the ridge plan has been revised by subsequent mapping; the geometry is now generally described as C-shaped or partial concentric rings rather than a regular octagon.
- The radiocarbon date from Jaketown (ca. 400 B.C.) is now known to be too young for the Poverty Point occupation; modern dating places the main occupation at ca. 1700-1100 BCE.
- Ford's Hopewell connection has been definitively rejected by chronological evidence.
