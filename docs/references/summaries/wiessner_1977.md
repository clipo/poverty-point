## Relevance to Argument

Wiessner 1977 is the dissertation underlying every subsequent Wiessner paper on hxaro and the most-cited single source for !Kung exchange-network detail. The audit purpose for this entry was to test whether the "10-30% additional resources during shortfalls" figure that earlier drafts of Paper 1 attributed to Wiessner-source ethnographic anchoring originates here. Within the reading scope (front matter, theoretical-framework opening, Chapter IV food-sharing section), the figure was not located. The chapters not exhaustively read contain quantitative tables on hxaro partner distribution, resource mapping, and possessions, but their titles do not announce a shortfall-resource-access percentage as a finding. The four-source audit chain (Hawkes 2000, Wiessner 1982, Wiessner 2002, Wiessner 1977) consistently fails to locate the 10-30% figure. Paper 1 has already been revised away from imputing the figure to any specific source. The dissertation remains the canonical primary citation for the qualitative claim that hxaro is the dominant shortfall-buffering mechanism among Ju/'hoansi.

## Data Presented

- **Sample**: 80 adults at /ai/ai and Dobe (random-sample interviews on hxaro); 200-300 !Xo at Bere and Lonetree (cross-linguistic comparison); !Kung from Caprivi to Nxau Nxau (regional sample); 5,000 San in Ghanzi farm block (broader survey).
- **Geographic scope**: !Kung in northwestern Botswana, !Xo at Bere and Lonetree, /Gwi and G//ana in Central Kalahari Game Reserve, /Kung in Bushmanland (Namibia), and others.
- **Time depth**: 1973-1975 fieldwork (two and a half years), supplemented by Lee's prior censuses (1963-1969) and Marshall's earlier work.
- **Quantitative tables**: 18 tables documenting !Kung population structure (Table 1), hxaro partner distribution (Tables 7-15), demographic change at /ai/ai 1964-1974 (Table 16), and possessions comparisons (Tables 17-18).
- **Hxaro-network data**: 955 partnerships among 59 adults and 14 children (the figure that subsequent Wiessner papers reference back to this dissertation).

## Methodological Notes

- The dissertation was originally framed as a study of stylistic variation in artifacts in relation to social organization (Wiessner's preface details this trajectory). The hxaro analysis emerged as a necessary precondition for stylistic interpretation when it became clear that artifacts circulate widely through hxaro chains and cannot be analyzed at point of recovery without understanding the network.
- Wiessner spent two and a half months in intensive interviewing on hxaro, with 80 adults at /ai/ai and Dobe, after a year and a half of relationship-building.
- Conversation tabulation: 40% food, 20% gossip about wealth-hoarding, 40% other — yielding the "60% of conversation involves resource-redistribution social control" finding (p. xxxv).
- Cross-cultural comparison in Chapter VIII situates the !Kung case against other hunter-gatherer reciprocity systems.
